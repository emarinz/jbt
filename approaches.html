Julia Brown, LMHC

Psychotherapy for Individuals and Couples

Approaches

I provide psychotherapy for adults and couples who wish to have a closer connection with self and others and a greater sense of clarity and purpose, or are seeking healing and renewal. I engage in a creative dialogue with patients, exploring difficulties that may be influenced by long-standing challenges and patterns of behavior; in pursuit of new ways of thinking and feeling. Together we can address issues that are troubling you as well as identifying concerns from the past that may be standing in the way of a more fulfilling life.

My practice reflects an integration of training and experience with psychodynamic, mindfulness and cognitive behavioral interventions. My approach is rooted in the therapeutic relationship; compassionate, client centered and supportive.

I work with issues such as those listed below:

  • life stages and transitions (separation/divorce, job/career change, getting older)
  • work identity/career planning
  • professional growth, work stressors and conflicts, balancing work and personal life
  • depression and anxiety, grief and loss
  • relationships, interpersonal difficulties, family conflict
  • issues stemming from family of origin/past relationships
  • parenting (from pregnancy through adult children)
  • body and eating issues
  • personal identity, growth and self-actualization
  • creativity and the cultivation of self-expression

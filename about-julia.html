About Julia

I am an interpersonal psychotherapist and use a supportive relational approach through which we can address your life stressors, explore your thoughts and feelings and articulate your goals. My interest is in working with individuals and couples to foster self-understanding and empowerment. I tailor my therapeutic approach to the unique needs and circumstances of each patient, maintaining a belief in each individualâ€™s capacity for positive change and personal growth.

I hold a Masters Degree in Mental Health and Wellness from NYU and began my work as a psychotherapist at a College Mental Health Counseling Center working with undergraduate and graduate students. I went on to work at a Community Mental Health Clinic, a Psychoanalytic Training Institute and a Group Psychotherapy Practice which focused on working with women through the life cycle. I have postgraduate training in psychodynamic psychotherapy, cognitive behavioral therapy and mindfulness based stress reduction.

Prior to becoming a therapist I worked in the art world as a museum curator and director and have an in depth understanding of the creative life. My work is deeply influenced by the unique nature of the creative process. I am committed to helping individuals deepen their self-knowledge and understanding, find their own voice, and connect with their authentic selves, both within, and in relationship to others.
